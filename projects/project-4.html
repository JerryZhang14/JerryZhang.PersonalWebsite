Project 04

Automated Water Pre-Treatment System

Developed as a solution for water safety in remote communities, this automated plant integrates chemical dispensing, mechanical mixing, and multi-stage filtration. The project focuses on a reliable "Set and Forget" design for pre-treating surface water.

Every custom component—from the pivoting alum dispenser to the curved-blade impeller—was modeled from scratch in Autodesk Inventor. These parts were then 3D printed, assembled onto a mechatronic chassis, and driven by an Arduino MEGA through a rigorous testing phase to ensure precise 0.8g dosing and clear water output.

Design & Development Process

01

Inventor CAD Modeling

  • Designed the primary dispensing assembly with a pivoting mechanism to prevent alum powder bridging.
  • Modeled the internal impeller with a 4-blade curved design to maximize turbulence for flocculation.
02

3D Printing & Fabrication

  • Optimized print orientation for structural integrity and waterproofing.
  • Post-processed components for magnetic coupling between the external motor and internal mixer.
03

Assembly & Mechatronics

  • Integrated turbidity sensors and submersible pumps into the Arduino-controlled chassis.
  • Applied silicone sealing to all mechatronic-fluid interfaces to ensure safety and system longevity.
04

System Testing

  • Iteratively tested the FSM logic to handle edge cases like empty chemical hoppers or sensor failure.
  • Measured sediment removal rates against baseline turbidity to confirm safe potable water output.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Leak Prevention: Solved by using magnetic coupling, eliminating the need for a drive-shaft hole in the tank.
  • Dispensing Accuracy: Iterated on the hopper design in CAD to ensure a consistent 0.8g dose per cycle.

Final Outcome

A fully validated prototype that automates complex water treatment chemistry into a single-button operation, meeting all functional requirements for remote community deployment.
